AI ON THUMBS

As a part of the AI Camp's incubator program, I worked on a team of 5 as both a UI/UX Designer, and front-end engineer to revamp and add new lessons to their AI on Thumbs app. The app serves to make learning about machine learning and artificial intelligence more accessible, addressing the issue of unequal access to education and costly nature of learning materials.

The program consisted of weekly meetings presenting progress to the rest of the company, live team collaboration, and utilization of sprint boards and other industry standard methodologies. With my teammate, I used Figma to design a new quiz functionality for the app, a new landing page, and 2 new lessons on neural networks. Then, using React Native, Expo, and Android Studio, I fully implemented the quiz functionality along with some of the lesson pages.

JAICE

JAICE is a web-based empathetic response generator/"therapy" chatbot I worked on for AI Camp's summer session. My team and I wanted to create something that resonated with some of our personal struggles and that many people would be able to utilize and interact with.

As the lead backend developer, I focused on cleaning our dataset of ~25,000 emotionally driven conversations using pandas, fine tuning a pre-trained 124M version of GPT-2 with aitextgen, and linking the model to our user-facing website.

The project took around 4 weeks and was successfully completed with the help of my team members and my mentor (listed on the Github page).

SEAING QUEER

SEAing Queer is a mapping project which aims to share the unique experiences of Southeast Asian LGBTQ+ UCLA students, through voluntary survey data. I worked on this project along with my partner Kienna for ASIAAM191A. In order to implement the goals we came up with together, I utilized largely open source technologies.

HTML, CSS, JS: Framework used to design and script the webpage MapLibreGL: Used to generate map based on JSON data, along with markers and popups. MapTiler: Used for API Key to produce map interface. PapaParse: Used to parse data from Google Sheets into a JSON format for further JavaScript processing.

This project is still ongoing, as we hope to refine the design of the webpage and more importantly, engage more of the Queer Southeast Asian community on campus and work with campus organizations to give back our findings to empower these communities.
